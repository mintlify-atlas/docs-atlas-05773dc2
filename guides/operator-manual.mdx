## How to Access the System

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the browser">
    Open your web browser and enter the address given by your supervisor
    
    Example: `http://192.168.1.10` or `https://mes.yourworkshop.com`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    Type your **email** and **password** on the login screen
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Login">
    Click **"Login"** to access the system
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If you forgot your password, ask the owner or supervisor to reset it for you.
</Tip>

---

## Main Screen — "My Shift"

When you log in, you see the **"My Shift"** screen.

This shows **only the work orders assigned to you**.

### What You'll See

Each work order card displays:

- **Code** (e.g., `OT-0042`)
- **Title** of the job
- **Client** name
- **Status** badge with color
- **Delivery date** committed to the client
- **Action buttons** (Start, Pause, Finish)

<Note>
You will NOT see orders assigned to other operators — only your own work.
</Note>

---

## Work Order Statuses and Colors

Each status has a specific color to help you quickly identify what action is needed:

| Status | Color | Meaning |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **PLANNED** | Blue | You've been assigned this order, but haven't started yet |
| **PENDING** | Gray | Waiting for you to begin |
| **IN PROCESS** | Green | You're currently working on it |
| **PAUSED** | Red | You temporarily stopped work |
| **FINISHED** | Dark Green | Work completed |

## Step-by-Step: How to Work an Order

### ▶ Step 1 — Starting a Work Order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the order">
    Locate the work order in your "My Shift" list
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify status">
    Make sure the status is **PLANNED** or **PENDING**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Start">
    Click the **▶ Start** button
  </Step>
  <Step title="Status changes">
    The status changes to **IN PROCESS** (green) ✅
    
    The system records the exact start time automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
You can only start ONE work order at a time. If you already have one in process, the supervisor must manage the transition.
</Warning>

---

### ⏸ Step 2 — Pausing (If You Need to Stop)

If you need to stop temporarily (lunch break, material shortage, etc.):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the active order">
    Locate the work order that's **IN PROCESS**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Pause">
    Click the **⏸ Pause** button
  </Step>
  <Step title="Status changes to Paused">
    The status changes to **PAUSED** (red)
    
    The system logs the pause time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Resuming a Paused Order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the paused order">
    Locate the order with **PAUSED** status (red badge)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Resume">
    Click the **▶ Resume** button
  </Step>
  <Step title="Back to In Process">
    The status returns to **IN PROCESS** (green)
    
    You can continue working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

### ✅ Step 3 — Finishing a Work Order

When you complete the work:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your work">
    Make sure the job is actually finished according to specifications
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Finish">
    Click the **✅ Finish** button
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional: Add closing note">
    A confirmation dialog appears. You can add a **closing note** (optional)
    
    Example: "Finished according to specification" or "Minor adjustment made to tolerance"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **"Confirm"**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Order marked finished">
    The system records the exact finish time. The order moves to **FINISHED** status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Make sure to finish the order only when the work is **truly complete**. This time is used for productivity calculations and client billing.
</Warning>

## Registering Material Consumption

While a work order is **IN PROCESS**, you can register materials you've used:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Expand the order details">
    Click **"📦 Materials and Notes"** button next to the work order
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Register Consumption section">
    Scroll to the **"📦 Register Consumption"** panel
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the material">
    Use the dropdown to search for the material (e.g., "steel rod", "paint", "welding wire")
    
    The dropdown shows current stock levels.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter quantity">
    Type the **quantity** you used (e.g., 2.5 meters, 10 units)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional: Add observation">
    Add a note if needed (e.g., "Used extra due to initial defect")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Register">
    Click **"+ Register Physical Consumption"**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consumption saved">
    The material consumption is recorded with timestamp and appears in the list below
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Registering materials is very important because it allows the supervisor and owner to know the **real cost** of the job.
</Info>

### Viewing Registered Consumptions

Below the registration form, you'll see a list of **"Previously registered consumptions"** showing:

- Time of consumption
- Material name
- Quantity with unit

## Adding Notes or Comments

You can leave notes about a work order at any time:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Expand order details">
    Click **"📦 Materials and Notes"** for the work order
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Operations Log section">
    Find the **"💬 Operations Log and Updates"** panel
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your comment">
    In the text area, type your note
    
    Example: "Material X was missing, notified supervisor" or "Tool wear detected, changed bit for second pass"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Save">
    Click **"Record in Log"** button
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note is saved">
    The note is recorded with your name and exact timestamp. The supervisor can see it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Use notes to communicate important information to your supervisor:
- Quality issues
- Material problems
- Tool or machine issues
- Suggestions for improvement
</Tip>

### Viewing Previous Notes

Below the comment form, you'll see the **"Operational History"** showing:

- Your previous notes and comments
- Timestamps
- Your name

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Why don't I see any work orders?">
  The supervisor hasn't assigned you any work yet. Check with your supervisor.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I see other operators' work orders?">
  No. You only see your own assigned orders. This keeps the interface simple and focused.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I make a mistake registering material consumption?">
  Notify the supervisor immediately so they can correct it from their interface. You cannot delete or edit consumptions.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Does the session expire?">
  Yes, after 8 hours you'll need to log in again. This is a security feature.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use this on a mobile phone?">
  Yes! The system is mobile-responsive. You can use it on a phone or tablet in the workshop.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if the internet goes down?">
  The system requires internet/network connection. If connection is lost, wait until it's restored and continue working. Time may need manual correction by supervisor.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I change my password?">
  Currently operators cannot change their own password. Ask your supervisor or the system owner to reset it.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I pause multiple times?">
  Yes. You can pause and resume as many times as needed. Each pause and resume is logged with timestamps.
</Accordion>

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start orders on time">
    Don't let PLANNED orders sit idle. Start them as soon as you're ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Be accurate with timing">
    Click Start/Pause/Finish at the exact moment you begin/stop work. This ensures accurate time tracking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register ALL materials">
    Record every material you use, no matter how small. This affects cost calculations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add helpful notes">
    Document issues, observations, and important details. Your supervisor and future operators will thank you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check delivery dates">
    Pay attention to delivery dates. If you see a red "delayed" badge, notify your supervisor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review before finishing">
    Double-check your work before clicking Finish. Make sure quality standards are met.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<Accordion title="Button is disabled/grayed out">
  The button is disabled because:
  - You're trying to start an order but already have one IN PROCESS
  - The order status doesn't allow that action
  - Network connection was lost (check your connection)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Material not found in dropdown">
  The material hasn't been added to the system yet. Ask your supervisor to add it to the Materials catalog.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cannot see order details">
  Click the "📦 Materials and Notes" button to expand the details panel. Make sure you're looking at the correct order.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Page is loading forever">
  - Check your internet/network connection
  - Refresh the page (F5 or reload button)
  - If problem persists, contact your supervisor
</Accordion>
